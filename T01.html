1.1 Plant tissues.

Meristematic tissue

  • The tissues with cells that divide actively by mitosis to produce new cells are called meristematic tissues.
  • These cells are not differentiated.
  • The growth of plants takes place due to the activity of meristematic tissues.
  • This tissue consists of small-sized living cells
  • No intercellular spaces or intercellular spaces are not prominent
  • There is a distinct nucleus in each cell
  • Absence of large central vacuole but small vacuoles may be present
  • A large number of mitochondria are present
  • There are 3 types
    1. Apical meristems
    2. Intercalary meristems.
    3. Lateral meristems.

Apical meristems

  • Apical meristems are found in
      1. Shoot apex.
      2. Root apex.
      3. Axillary buds.
  • The plant increases its height due to the activity of this tissue

Intercalary meristems

  • Intercalary meristems are found at nodes.
  • This tissue consists of small-sized living cells
  • The length of the internode increases due to the activity of the above tissue.
  • There is a distinct nucleus in each cell
  • They are found in plants of the grass family

Lateral meristems

  • Lateral meristems are present laterally in the stem and roots of a plant.
  • They are found parallel to the longitudinal axis of the plant.
  • The diameter of the plant increases due to the activity of this tissue.
  • Cambium tissue found in dicots is a lateral meristematic tissue.

Permanent tissues


Simple permanent

  • The simple permanent tissue is composed of similar cells.
  • There are 3 types
      1. parenchyma
      2. Sclerenchyma
      3. Collenchyma

Parenchyma

  • The tissue that forms the soft parts of the plant body is the parenchyma tissue.
  • Parenchyma tissue consists of living cells.
  • Cells are isodiametric (spherical) with a large central vacuole
  • The nucleus is present peripherally in the cytoplasm
  • The cell wall is thin and made up of cellulose.
  • Intercellular spaces are present.
  • Locations of parenchyma tissues
      1. Cortex and pith of the plantstem.
      2. Pith and.corter of roots
      3. Fleshy Parts of fruits
      4. Seeds (endosperm).
      5. Leaves (mesophyll).
  • Functions of parenchyma
      1. Photosynthesis
      2. Food Storage
      3. Storage of water
      4. providing Support.

collenchyma

  • Collenchyma tissue provides mechanical strength and support to the plant body.
  • They are modified parenchyma cells.
  • Collenchyma tissue consists of living cells
  • Cells possess a
      1. Cytoplasm.
      2. nucleus
      3. Central vacuoles.
  • Generally, cells are elongated and polygonal in cross-section
  • The corners of the cell walls are thickened.
  • Intercellular spaces may present or may not present
  • Locations of collenchymas
      1. The epidermis of herbaceous Stems..
      2. Veins of a dicot. leaves
  • Functions of collenchyma
      1. Provides mechanical support to dicot plant stems
      2. Photosynthesis

Sclerenchyma

  • Sclerenchyma tissue helps in providing mechanical strength and support to the plant body
  • This tissue has two types of cells
      1. Sclereids
      2. Sclerenchyma fibers
  • Sclerenchyma tissue consists of dead cells
  • Lignin is deposited on the cellulose cell wall
